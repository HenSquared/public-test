# Poems

## Poem 1: The River's Song

The river hums a silver tune,
Beneath the glow of a crescent moon,
It winds through meadows, rocks, and bends,
A song that starts but never ends.

Each stone it meets becomes a drum,
A rhythm soft, a gentle hum,
The willow trails her fingers long,
And sways in time with the river's song.

Through valleys carved by ancient rain,
It carries loss, it carries gain,
A thousand secrets, cold and deep,
The things the restless waters keep.

So listen close when evenings fall,
The river answers every call—
Of seasons past and those to come,
Of all we lose and all we've won.

## Poem 2: Morning Light

Before the world has learned to speak,
Before the sunrise paints the peak,
The morning holds its quiet breath,
Between the night and what comes next.

A single bird begins the song,
Then two, then three—a joyful throng,
The dew sits still on blades of grass,
Like tiny worlds in polished glass.

The light arrives in gentle waves,
It warms the hills and warms the caves,
It coaxes colour from the grey,
And slowly pulls the dark away.

This is the hour the heart feels free,
From all it was and yet could be,
When every dawn becomes a door—
A chance to try, to live once more.
